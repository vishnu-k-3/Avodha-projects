A P J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, often referred to as the "People's President" and the "Missile Man of India".

Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He came from a humble background and overcame financial challenges to pursue his education in aerospace engineering. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's space and defense programs. He was a key figure in the development of the country's first satellite launch vehicle and the ballistic missile program. His significant contributions earned him the title of "Missile Man of India." In 2002, Abdul Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India, serving until 2007. During his presidency, he focused on promoting education, particularly in the fields of science and technology, and inspiring the youth to dream big. Kalam was known for his simplicity, humility, and his ability to connect with people of all ages. After his term as president, Kalam continued to engage in educational and inspirational activities. Unfortunately, he passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. Despite his departure, Abdul Kalam's legacy lives on, and he remains an iconic figure in Indian history.
